# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A solo founder exploring the startup idea landscape. They use Scout in focused, often late-evening sessions — scanning signals, evaluating opportunities, looking for the next insight. They're technical enough to appreciate craft but want the tool to feel alive, not clinical. This is a personal instrument, not enterprise software.

## Product Purpose

Scout surfaces potential startup ideas by scraping community discussions (Reddit, HN, Twitter, review sites) for unsolved problems and pain points. It scores each idea across problem quality, market viability, sentiment signal, and founder-market fit — turning raw internet chatter into a prioritized, filterable pipeline of opportunities. Success means the founder finds an idea they wouldn't have otherwise seen, or gains conviction about one they were already considering.

## Brand Personality

**Adventurous, exploratory, premium.** Three words: *expedition*, *craft*, *discovery*.

Scout should feel like an explorer's instrument — a star chart for startup ideas. The interface treats each idea as a potential discovery, not a database row. There's warmth in the darkness (late-night expedition energy), precision in the scoring (serious craft underneath), and a sense of possibility throughout (every scroll might reveal *the one*).

The emotional goal is *focused anticipation* — the quiet excitement of prospecting, not the dopamine of a feed.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: blue sidebar, white cards, chart widgets, cookie-cutter data-viz. Scout is not a business analytics tool.
- **Bloomberg Terminal density**: overwhelming grids of numbers with no personality.
- **GitHub dark utilitarian**: the current codebase uses GitHub's dark palette. Scout should move away from cold blue-grays and dev-tool severity toward warmer, more adventurous dark tones.
- **Startup-landing-page tropes**: no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero-metric templates, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows.

## Design Principles

1. **Expedition, not administration.** Every surface should feel like exploring territory, not managing a database. Discovery is the core experience.

2. **Warmth in the dark.** Dark mode that doesn't feel cold — amber, deep ochre, warm neutrals. The interface should feel like a late-night study lit by a single lamp.

3. **Craft signals seriousness.** The scoring engine is rigorous; the interface should match that rigor with precision in typography, spacing, and motion. No slop.

4. **Signal over noise.** Data-dense when it needs to be, but curated. The interface edits — it doesn't just dump. Every element earns its place.

5. **Personal, not personalizable.** This is a founder's instrument. It should feel like *theirs*, not like configurable enterprise software. Opinionated defaults, not preference panels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable
- Reduced-motion support for all animations and transitions
- Color is never the sole channel for meaning (verdicts have text labels alongside color badges)
- Focus indicators visible and consistent
